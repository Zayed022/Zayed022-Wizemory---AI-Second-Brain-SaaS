# WizeMory — 3-Minute Loom Demo Script

> **Format:** Screen recording with face cam (Loom)
> **Total runtime:** 3:00 minutes
> **Target:** Potential acquirers — founders, PEs, and strategic buyers
> **Pre-requisites:** 6–8 real items saved in your account. Dashboard open on `/dashboard`. Knowledge graph tab open in a second tab.

---

## SETUP BEFORE RECORDING

- [ ] Browser: Chrome, 1280×720, no extensions visible except WizeMory
- [ ] Dashboard: `localhost:3000/dashboard` or `wizemory.com/dashboard`
- [ ] Pre-saved items: 2 articles, 1 YouTube video, 1 note (already in READY state)
- [ ] Second tab: `/dashboard/graph` (not yet visible)
- [ ] Third tab: `/dashboard/agent` (not yet visible)
- [ ] Fourth tab: `/founder` (not yet visible)
- [ ] Close Slack, Gmail, all notifications
- [ ] Face cam: top-right corner, good lighting

---

## THE SCRIPT

---

### [0:00–0:20] — THE HOOK

*[Face cam visible. Dashboard in background. Speak to camera first.]*

**SPEAK:**

> "This is WizeMory. It took 3 months to build, it runs on $0 per month in infrastructure,
> and it does something no other tool does: it turns everything you read, watch, or write
> into a permanent, searchable, connected knowledge base — automatically.
>
> I'm going to show you three things in the next 3 minutes.
> The magic moment. The power move. And the numbers."

*[Click to dashboard — keep face cam on]*

---

### [0:20–1:00] — THE MAGIC MOMENT
#### "Paste a YouTube link. Watch what happens."

*[Dashboard is visible. Show the quick-add bar at the top.]*

**SPEAK:**

> "Watch this. I'm going to paste a YouTube link I've never saved before."

*[Click the YouTube quick-action button in the add bar. Paste: `https://youtube.com/watch?v=V-UvSKe8jW4` or any real video URL you have ready.]*

**SPEAK:**

> "That's it. That's all I had to do."

*[Watch the item appear with status "Processing…" amber badge]*

**SPEAK:**

> "In the background, Gemini AI is reading the full transcript of that video.
> Not the title — the entire content. Watch this card."

*[Wait 10–15 seconds for READY status. The card updates with real title, summary, and key insights.]*

**SPEAK:**

> "There. In 15 seconds: a 3-sentence summary, 3 specific actionable insights, and relevant tags —
> all extracted automatically, all connected to everything I've previously saved."

*[Switch to the Knowledge Graph tab — `/dashboard/graph`]*

**SPEAK:**

> "And here is where it gets interesting. This is my knowledge graph.
> Every node is something I've saved. Every edge is a connection — either a shared topic,
> or an AI-discovered thematic link between two ideas I'd never have connected myself."

*[Zoom into the graph slightly. Point to some clusters.]*

**SPEAK:**

> "The video I just saved has already appeared here, and WizeMory has already found
> that it connects to two other things I saved last week about the same theme.
> I didn't create any of this. The AI did — automatically, the moment I pasted that link."

---

### [1:00–1:45] — THE POWER MOVE
#### "Run an Agentic task."

*[Switch to the Agent tab — `/dashboard/agent`]*

**SPEAK:**

> "Now let me show you what makes this technically different from every other tool.
> This is WizeMory's AI Agent — a multi-step task runner built on Gemini's native function calling.
> Not prompt hacking. Actual tool use."

*[Click on the "Research → LinkedIn post" example workflow to pre-fill the goal textarea.]*

**SPEAK:**

> "I'm going to give it a complex, multi-step goal."

*[Edit the goal to something relevant to your saved content, like:
"Search my knowledge base for everything about AI and productivity, ask it what patterns you see, then write a LinkedIn post sharing my 3 best insights."]*

*[Click "Run agent"]*

**SPEAK:**

> "Watch the execution trace. It's not just generating text — it's breaking the goal into steps,
> choosing the right tool for each step, executing them in sequence, and feeding each result
> into the next step."

*[As steps appear, point to them]*

**SPEAK:**

> "Step 1: searching my knowledge base. Step 2: asking it a question. Step 3: generating the post.
> Three distinct AI tool calls, chained automatically."

*[Final answer appears]*

**SPEAK:**

> "And here is a LinkedIn post built entirely from my own research — not the internet's.
> It cites ideas I saved. It sounds like me. Because it is based on what I've actually read."

---

### [1:45–2:30] — THE CLOSING
#### "Here are the numbers."

*[Switch to the Founder dashboard tab — `/founder`]*

**SPEAK:**

> "Now let's talk about why this is worth $100,000."

*[Scroll through the founder dashboard slowly. Show the valuation banner.]*

**SPEAK:**

> "This is the founder dashboard. Real metrics. Live data.
> Conservative acquisition value shown at the top — based on current user count, ARR, activation rate."

*[Point to the funnel]*

**SPEAK:**

> "The conversion funnel shows what a buyer actually cares about: signups to activated users,
> activated to engaged, engaged to paid. These are the numbers that determine SaaS multiples."

*[Scroll to the weekly signups chart]*

**SPEAK:**

> "Week-over-week growth. And a 12-month revenue projection assuming 10% monthly growth —
> which is conservative for a product with zero CAC and a built-in viral loop."

*[Scroll to "Why acquirers buy WizeMory" section]*

**SPEAK:**

> "Here is the thesis: $0 infrastructure cost. Viral acquisition built in. Deep retention moat —
> after 3 months of use, switching cost approaches infinity.
> And a B2B upside nobody has touched yet — the Team plan at $49 per user per month."

---

### [2:30–3:00] — THE CLOSE

*[Switch back to face cam only, or face cam + dashboard in background]*

**SPEAK:**

> "What you're looking at is 3 months of engineering, deployed in production, running on zero dollars
> per month in infrastructure, with a 95% gross margin on every subscription.
>
> The code is clean TypeScript, fully documented, with a 30-day handoff plan included.
> A senior developer can add a new feature in 2 hours — I've measured it.
>
> The asking price is $100,000.
>
> That's less than what it would cost you to hire one engineer for one year,
> and you'd get a production SaaS with AI pipelines, a knowledge graph, an agentic task runner,
> GDPR compliance, a browser extension, and a path to $1M ARR already mapped.
>
> The demo link, the founder dashboard, and the due diligence document are all in the description.
> If you want to talk, email is in the listing."

*[Hold on face for 2 seconds. Smile. End recording.]*
